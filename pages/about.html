I chose to pursue a degree in architecture because I believed that buildings were the foundation of a thriving community. Growing up, I watched the buildings around me decay and crumble and my community dwindle. After attending the University of Oklahoma for 4 years, I realize there is much more to the discipline of architecture and a variety of ways that I can influence the built environment. For me, architecture is a method of creating spaces and experiences that influence a population. It is about proposing a solution to a problem through design and innovation. Architecture is about researching a place, the site, the community, the local government, and understanding what defines it. Architecture has the capability to encourage new economies and new spaces for cultural or social exchange. To bring all these pieces together, I rigorously work to understand the context of any project through many iterations and research.
